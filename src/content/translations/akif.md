tagline: The one we are named »Kifić« after
---

Akif, or Kifo as he was called, was one of Avdikadija's four sons. It is after him that the descendants of his sons Avdo and [Himzo](/en/people/himzo) in Jezero — known as Avdo Kifin and Himzo Kifin — came to be called Kifić.

He was married to [Hanumica](/en/people/hanumica), one of the four daughters of Smailbeg Ganibegović of Vražić. With her he had four children: sons Avdo and Himzo and daughters [Bisera](/en/people/bisera) and [Paša](/en/people/pasa). Their gravestone records that Avdo was older than his brother Himzo.

Akif's exact occupation is unknown, but he was a wealthy man in Jezero: he left his sons a large estate and a great mill with twelve stones, gave Himzo the terzija (tailoring) trade, and Avdo also had a han (inn) and two shops.

Akif lived through turbulent times. When Omer-pasha seized the sons of the Jezero kadi to force their father's surrender, Akif was among them; after Avdikadija surrendered the brothers were released. Bosnia remained restless afterwards as the Ottoman Empire weakened.
